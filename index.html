📈 Stock Market Basics

A stock is a small piece of ownership in a company. If the company grows, your share can become more valuable. If it struggles, your investment can fall.

What is a Stock?

  • You own a small part of the company
  • Share price can rise or fall daily
  • Best suited for long-term investors

How Does it Trade?

  • Bought and sold on NSE/BSE
  • Price changes due to demand and supply
  • Short-term movement is normal

How Do You Make Money?

Capital Gains: Buy at ₹100, sell at ₹150 → ₹50 profit.
Dividends: Some companies share profits with shareholders periodically.

Risks to Know

Stock prices can fall sharply. Individual companies can go bankrupt. Short-term volatility is normal — the market always moves up and down.

💡 Real-Life Example

Imagine Zomato IPO in 2021. Early investors bought at ₹76. Even though it fell initially, patient investors who held through understood the business and saw recovery. Stock investing rewards patience and research.

🧺 Mutual Funds

Mutual funds pool money from many investors and spread it across stocks or bonds, making them easier and safer for beginners than selecting single stocks.

Types of Mutual Funds

  • Equity: higher growth, higher volatility
  • Debt: more stable, lower return
  • Hybrid: balance between both

What is NAV?

  • NAV is one unit's price
  • It changes daily
  • You invest based on units, not share count

Expense Ratio

The annual fee the fund charges (usually 0.1%–2.5%). Lower is better. Index funds typically have very low expense ratios (~0.1%).

Why Mutual Funds?

No need to research individual stocks. Professionally managed. Highly diversified. You can start with just ₹500/month via SIP.

💡 Real-Life Example

Parag Parikh Flexi Cap Fund has delivered ~18% annual returns over 5 years. If you invested ₹5000/month via SIP for 5 years (₹3 lakh total), you'd have approximately ₹4.8 lakh — a gain of ₹1.8 lakh just by staying consistent.

🔄 SIP — Systematic Investment Plan

SIP means investing a fixed amount every month. It keeps investing simple, reduces timing mistakes and makes compounding easier over the long run.

How SIP Works

  • A fixed amount is invested every month
  • You buy more units when prices are lower
  • Consistency matters more than timing

Rupee Cost Averaging

  • Down market: buy more units
  • Up market: buy fewer units
  • Average cost improves over time

Power of Compounding

Your returns earn returns. ₹500/month at 12% for 20 years = ₹4.99 lakh total invested → ~₹49 lakh at maturity. That's the magic of compounding.

Starting a SIP

Download Groww or Zerodha. Complete KYC (Aadhaar + PAN). Choose a fund. Set monthly amount & date. Done — takes ~15 minutes!

💡 Real-Life Example

Riya, 22, starts a SIP of ₹2000/month in a Nifty 50 Index Fund at 12% average return. By age 42 (20 years), her ₹4.8 lakh investment grows to ~₹19.8 lakh. She beat inflation, built an emergency corpus, and never had to think about timing the market.

⚖️ Risk vs Return

Higher return potential usually comes with higher risk. The right choice depends on how long you can stay invested and how much volatility you can handle.

Low Risk Investments

  • Examples: FD, PPF, Debt Funds
  • Returns: around 4-7%
  • Safer, but may not beat inflation

Medium Risk Investments

  • Examples: Hybrid or balanced funds
  • Returns: around 10-14%
  • Good for 3-7 year goals

High Risk Investments

Stocks, Small-cap Funds
Return: 15–25%+ annually
Risk: High volatility. Can lose 30-50% in bad years.

Diversification

Don't put all eggs in one basket. Spread across asset classes — equity + debt + gold — to reduce risk without sacrificing too much return.

💡 Risk Rule of Thumb

Subtract your age from 100 to get your ideal equity allocation. At 25: 75% in equity, 25% in debt. At 50: 50% equity, 50% debt. As you age, protect your wealth by shifting to safer assets.
